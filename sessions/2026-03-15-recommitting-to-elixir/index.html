Recommitting to Elixir

Ben Miller

March 15, 2026

After much thought and research, I’ve decided to commit to Elixir until the completion of this project. Possibly with some Gleam mixed in if it makes domain modeling easier, but trying to avoid even that until I run into things that are unreasonably hard to do in Elixir.

What we have now mostly works, but there are some pretty critical bugs indicating deeper problems. I don’t trust that the LLM is just going to figure these out. What’s more, I don’t have much of a clue about what we’ve written so far — which is why I’ve spent the last few days reading up on the Elixir ecosystem and internals. I’m by no means an expert yet, but at least I now have a vague idea of how to explore the codebase and architect things. I’ll still mostly rely on the LLM to do most of the work, but at least now I have some mental model of what’s going on.

One principle I’d like to follow going forward: stick to what’s working. If something isn’t a problem, or isn’t directly connected to a deeper problem, lean towards leaving it alone. There’s always going to be un-pretty code sitting around, and I’ll waste a lot of time if I try to clean everything up before proceeding. Only clean up what pertains to the change at hand. Maybe at some point an architecture/code quality audit will be in order, but we’re not there yet.

Goals for this session:

A few very concerning behaviors are standing out to me: columns in the weekly PDF sometimes showing out of order. Is this a database issue? An error in the Typst? I’m genuinely surprised this is happening, and the fact that I don’t know where to look is a sign that I have almost no mental model of how the application works — which is exactly why I took some time to understand the underlying technology. Similarly, lots of tasks are intermittently showing up out of order within their individual routine blocks.

We could address these bugs individually, and that might be the right place to start. But this is also the right time to consider redesigning the routine model from scratch, in which case fixing those bugs would be redundant. So let’s leave them for now, but keep in mind whatever might be underlying them. Worth noting that we’ve already had several bugs resulting from database IDs not being configured or used correctly.

Before deciding what to change, I want to make sure I’ve answered my open questions about the current application:

  1. How are we interfacing with Ecto and the database?
  2. What does the boundary between the frontend (React) and the backend (Elixir) look like, and does it still make sense? When are we using channels vs HTTP, and does that make sense?
  3. How does it interface with Rust/GIL? Could that potentially take down the BEAM? Does it allow for multithreading, or is it a singleton?

For now: just help me answer these questions. After that, I’ll figure out what I want the next changes to be.
